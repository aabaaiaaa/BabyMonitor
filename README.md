# Baby Monitor

A free baby monitor that runs in your browser. No app to download, no account to create, no subscription.

Open it on two devices — one goes in the baby's room, one stays with you. Video and audio stream directly between your devices over your home Wi-Fi. Nothing is sent to the internet.

---

## What you need

- **Two devices** — phones, tablets, laptops, or any mix.
- **Google Chrome** on both devices.
- **The same Wi-Fi network** on both devices.

---

## Getting started

1. Open the app in Chrome on both devices.
2. On the device going in the baby's room, tap **Baby Monitor**.
3. On your device, tap **Parent Monitor**.
4. Follow the on-screen steps — you'll scan a QR code to connect the two devices.

The app remembers your devices after the first setup. Next time, it connects automatically.

---

## Soothing modes

Control what plays on the baby device from your phone:

| Mode | What plays on the baby device |
|---|---|
| Candle | Soft flickering candlelight |
| Water | Gentle blue rippling light |
| Stars | Drifting night sky with twinkling stars |
| Music | Audio tracks you upload from your phone |
| Combined | A light effect and music at the same time |
| Off | Screen off, no sound |

You can set a fade-out timer so the sound gradually stops after the baby falls asleep.

Soothing modes keep running even if your phone briefly loses connection — the devices reconnect on their own.

---

## Alerts

The app can notify you when:

- **Sound in the room** goes above a level you choose.
- **The baby device battery** is running low (below 20%).

Set how sensitive the sound alert is from the parent dashboard.

---

## Speaking to your baby

Tap the microphone button on the parent screen to speak through the baby device's speaker. Audio from your room is muted while you speak so there's no feedback.

---

## Multiple parents

Need a second person to monitor — a partner, grandparent, or nanny? You can add their device without touching the baby device again.

From the parent dashboard, tap **Share Access**, show the QR code on screen, and have them scan it on their device.

---

## Installing to your home screen

Adding the app to your home screen makes it open full-screen, like a regular app, and load instantly.

**Android:** Tap the three-dot menu in Chrome → **Add to Home screen**.

**iPhone / iPad:** Tap the Share button → **Add to Home Screen**.

**Laptop or desktop:** Click the install icon at the right of the Chrome address bar.

---

## "Tap anywhere to begin"

Every time you open the app you'll see a **"Tap anywhere to begin"** screen. This is not a bug — browsers require a tap before allowing access to the camera, microphone, or audio. Tap once and the app continues normally.

---

## iPhone and iPad

iPhones and iPads work, but with two things worth knowing:

**Light effects pause when the screen turns off.**
The Candle, Water, and Stars effects stop when an iPhone's screen goes to sleep. Music keeps playing. To work around this, either use Music mode on the baby device or go to **iPhone Settings → Display & Brightness → Auto-Lock** and set it to Never while the monitor is running.

**Alerts only appear while the app is on screen.**
Apple does not allow browsers to send notifications when the app is in the background. You won't get sound or banner alerts if the app isn't visible on your screen. If you need reliable background alerts, use an Android phone or laptop as the parent device.

---

## Privacy

- No account. No sign-up.
- Video and audio travel directly between your two devices — nothing passes through any server.
- Your settings and device history are saved on your devices only, not uploaded anywhere.

The one exception: when using Quick Pair (the default connection method), a free third-party service briefly helps the two devices find each other over the internet. Only connection details are exchanged — no audio or video. Once connected, everything goes directly between your devices.

To connect with no internet at all, choose **Offline QR** in the pairing screen.

## Troubleshooting

**The camera or microphone is blocked.**
Tap the lock icon in the Chrome address bar → set Camera and Microphone to Allow → reload the page.

**The devices won't connect.**
Make sure both are on the same Wi-Fi network (not one on Wi-Fi and one on mobile data). Try closing and reopening the app on both devices, then pair again. If Quick Pair keeps failing, try **Offline QR** — it works without any internet connection.

**No audio on the baby device.**
Make sure you tapped the "Tap to begin" screen on the baby device. Check the volume is not muted. If the problem persists, reload the page and try again.

**A banner says a new version is available.**
Tap **Update** — the page reloads with the latest version. This happens automatically when the app is improved.
