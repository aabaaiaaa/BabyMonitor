Settings

Default Preferences

These defaults are applied when a new baby monitor connects. Per-device thresholds are stored in each device's saved profile.

Speak-Through Mode

Choose how the speak-through microphone button works.

TURN Server (optional)

In strict NAT or corporate network environments where the default STUN-based connection fails, a TURN relay server lets the app connect via a relay. Leave blank to use the default configuration (STUN only).

Saved Devices

Previously paired baby monitors. Labels are automatically restored when a recognised device reconnects. You can rename or remove saved devices here.

Custom PeerJS Server (optional)

By default, Quick Pair uses the public PeerJS cloud signalling server. Configure a self-hosted PeerJS server here if you prefer not to rely on the public server. Changes take effect on the next connection attempt.

Alert Sounds

A gentle chime plays on this device when a noise threshold or low-battery alert fires — so you are notified even if you are not looking at the dashboard. Noise and battery alerts use distinct tones so you can tell them apart by sound.

This volume is separate from Monitor Volume and Music Volume.

Here's a quick overview of your parent dashboard:

  1. Live feed — watch and listen to your baby in real time. Tap the feed to open the control panel.
  2. Soothing modes — remotely start candle, water, or star light effects, or play music on the baby device.
  3. Speak-through — tap and hold the microphone button to talk to your baby. Your voice plays through the baby device's speaker.
  4. Alerts — receive notifications for movement, noise, and low battery — even when your screen is off.
  5. Add more devices — monitor up to 4 babies at once, or share the feed with another parent device using the + button.
